Understanding Single Collisions

Molecular Beams

The Experiment:

  • State Selection: Velocity, vibration, rotation.
  • Detection: Angular distribution and product states.

Backward Scattering

Potential Energy Surfaces (PES)

The landscape of chemical reactions.

  • Valleys: Reactants and Products.
  • Saddle Point: Transition State.
  • Reaction Path: Minimum Energy Path.

Energy Disposal

How is the available energy distributed?

$$E_{\text{avail}} = E_{\text{trans}} + E_{\text{vib}} + E_{\text{rot}}$$
Attractive Surface (Early Barrier):
Energy goes into Vibration.
(e.g., Harpoon Mechanism)
Repulsive Surface (Late Barrier):
Energy goes into Translation.
(e.g., H + Cl$_2$)

Polanyi's Rules

Rule 1 (Early Barrier): Translational energy is most effective for reaction.

Rule 2 (Late Barrier): Vibrational energy is most effective for reaction.

The Harpoon Mechanism

K + Br$_2$ $\to$ K$^+$ + Br$_2^-$ $\to$ KBr + Br

  1. Electron jump at long range ($\sim 6$ Å).
  2. Coulomb attraction pulls ions together.
  3. "Harpooning" the reactant.
  4. Results in vibrationally excited KBr.

Cross-Section:

Huge! ($\sim 200$ Å$^2$)
