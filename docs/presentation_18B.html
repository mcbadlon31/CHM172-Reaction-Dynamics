Diffusion-Controlled Reactions

Reaction Dynamics • Physical Chemistry

Reactions in Liquid Solutions

Gas vs. Liquid Phase

Gas Phase:
Molecules fly freely. Single collisions. Low density.
Liquid Phase:
Molecules are crowded. Cage Effect. Diffusion limits encounter rate.

The Encounter Pair

When A and B meet in solution, they are trapped in a solvent cage.

  • They undergo $\sim 100-1000$ collisions before separating.
  • This cluster is called an Encounter Pair.
Cage lifetime: $\sim 10^{-11}$ s
Collision freq in cage: $\sim 10^{13}$ s$^{-1}$

Two Limiting Regimes

Diffusion Control

Fast Reaction

  • Every encounter $\to$ Reaction
  • Rate limited by transport (diffusion)
  • $k \approx 10^{10}$ M$^{-1}$s$^{-1}$
  • Low $E_a$ ($\sim$ viscosity)

Activation Control

Slow Reaction

  • Many encounters, few reactions
  • Rate limited by energy barrier
  • $k \ll 10^{10}$ M$^{-1}$s$^{-1}$
  • High $E_a$

The Two-Step Mechanism

$$A + B \underset{k_{-d}}{\overset{k_d}{\rightleftharpoons}} [AB] \xrightarrow{k_a} P$$
  1. Diffusion Together ($k_d$): Formation of encounter pair.
  2. Diffusion Apart ($k_{-d}$): Breakup of encounter pair.
  3. Reaction ($k_a$): Chemical transformation within cage.

Steady-State Rate Law

Applying the steady-state approximation to [AB]:

$$k_{eff} = \frac{k_a k_d}{k_{-d} + k_a}$$

Resistance Form:

$$\frac{1}{k_{eff}} = \frac{1}{k_d} + \frac{1}{K k_a}$$

Like electrical resistors in series, the slowest step dominates.

Diffusion Theory

Fick's First Law:

$$J = -D \frac{\partial c}{\partial x}$$

Flux is proportional to concentration gradient.

Stokes-Einstein Relation:

$$D = \frac{k_B T}{6\pi \eta r}$$

Connects diffusion ($D$) to viscosity ($\eta$) and size ($r$).

Smoluchowski Limit

Deriving the maximum possible rate ($k_d$):

$$k_d = 4\pi R^* D N_A$$

Substituting Stokes-Einstein ($D \approx k_BT/6\pi\eta r$):

$$k_d \approx \frac{8RT}{3\eta}$$

$k_d \approx$ 7.4 × 109 M$^{-1}$s$^{-1}$

Temperature Dependence

For diffusion-controlled reactions:

$$k_d \propto \frac{T}{\eta(T)}$$

Since viscosity $\eta$ decreases with $T$, rate increases.

Apparent $E_a \approx 10-20$ kJ/mol (viscosity activation energy).

Experimental Verification

Reaction $k$ (M$^{-1}$s$^{-1}$) Type
H$^+$ + OH$^-$ $1.4 \times 10^{11}$ Fast (Grotthuss)
Radical Recombination $\sim 10^{10}$ Diffusion Limit
Enzyme-Substrate $\sim 10^8 - 10^9$ Near Limit
Sucrose Hydrolysis $10^{-5}$ Activation Controlled

Reaction-Diffusion Coupling

When reaction and diffusion compete:

$$\lambda = \sqrt{\frac{D}{k}}$$

$\lambda$ (Reaction-Diffusion Length):
Distance a molecule diffuses before reacting.

Important for biological patterns and catalysis.
